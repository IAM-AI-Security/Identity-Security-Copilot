# NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) Summary

This document summarizes the core components of the NIST AI RMF (AI 100-1), focusing on aspects relevant to AI identity and access governance.

## Core Functions

The AI RMF is structured around four core functions:

1. **Govern:**
   - Cultivate and implement a culture of risk management within organizations designing, developing, deploying, or acquiring AI systems.
   - Align AI risk management with broader enterprise risk management.
   - *Identity Governance Context:* Define ownership, accountability, and access control policies for AI models, training data, and execution environments.

2. **Map:**
   - Establish context to frame risks related to an AI system.
   - Understand the intended use, limits, and potential impacts of the AI system.
   - *Identity Governance Context:* Map the identities (human and non-human) that interact with the AI system and document their required permissions.

3. **Measure:**
   - Employ quantitative, qualitative, or mixed-method tools, techniques, and methodologies to analyze, assess, benchmark, and monitor AI risk and related impacts.
   - *Identity Governance Context:* Measure privilege drift, monitor access logs, and audit authentication mechanisms for AI agents.

4. **Manage:**
   - Allocate risk resources to mapped and measured risks on a regular basis and as defined by the Govern function.
   - *Identity Governance Context:* Implement automated revocation of unused access, enforce Just-In-Time (JIT) provisioning for AI execution roles, and apply boundary policies.

## Characteristics of Trustworthy AI

The framework identifies several characteristics of trustworthy AI systems:
- Valid and Reliable
- Safe
- Secure and Resilient
- Accountable and Transparent
- Explainable and Interpretable
- Privacy-Enhanced
- Fair with Harmful Bias Managed

*Security and Resilience* directly involve protecting the system from unauthorized access and adversarial attacks, necessitating robust identity governance controls.
